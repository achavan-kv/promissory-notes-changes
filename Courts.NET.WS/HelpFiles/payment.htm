Payment Screen

The Payments screen is used to take payments across the different account types (Cash, Hire Purchase, Ready Finance) belonging to a Customer.

 

Payments on combined Ready Finance accounts

 

  • If there is more than 1 Ready Finance account that has been delivered, these will appear as combined and any payment made will be split across all Ready Finance accounts (It will be a pro rata split).

  • Towards the right-hand side of the screen there is a button called ‘Combined Ready Finance Accounts’. Clicking on this button will enable you to view the individual details (such as Instalment, Outstanding Balance, Arrears, Payment) on each account.

  • The Details tab towards the right-hand side of the screen should display the combined details for the combined Ready Finance accounts (e.g. Outstanding Balance, Instalment Amount, Arrears).

  • If a payment amount is entered on the Payments screen which is < than the combined installment amount across the Ready Finance accounts, the payment should be correctly split in proportion to the installment amounts on each account, where an adjustment may be made to the most recently opened Ready Finance account to ensure that the correct payment is made. This can be seen by clicking on the 'Combined Ready Finance' button.

  • If a payment amount is entered  on the Payments screen which is = to the combined installment amount, the payment should be correctly split where the installment amount on each account is exactly paid. This can be seen by clicking on the 'Combined Ready Finance' button.

  • Through the ‘Ready Finance Combined’ screen for individual Ready Finance accounts, you are able to override a payment amount on any of the accounts by entering a value into the Payment field.

  • If the combined ‘Settlement Figure’ is entered into the Payment Amount field, then the individual settlement amounts should be paid on each account. This can be seen by viewing the split by clicking on the 'Combined Ready Finance' button.

  • If a Payment Amount is entered which covers the ‘Settlement Amount’ of some Ready Finance accounts, then the split will make sure that only the settlement amount is paid towards these accounts.

 

Payments on combined Ready Finance accounts allocated to Bailiffs

 

  • If an account is in arrears, is allocated to a bailiff and the debit account indicator is set to true for the bailiff (on Bailiff Commissions) screen then two new fields should appear in the Payments screen which are ‘Total Amount’ & ‘Credit Fee’.

  • Entering ‘0’ in the ‘Total Amount’ field should correctly calculate the whole payment amount that you would be needed to cover the arrears + fee amount on the accounts.

  • If there are multiple Ready Finance accounts allocated to different bailiffs, than you should note that the Fee on each individual account should be based on the commission rate of the Bailiff allocated to that account.

  • Please note that the Fee is calculated based on the status that the account was in when it was allocated to the Bailiff, and the commission percentage that applies to that status.

  • Please note that there is a country parameter ‘Fees calculated on Payment Amount’ (under Tax/Service Charges/Fees).  If this is set to True, then the minimum Part Payment is the part payment + fee amount needed to cover the minimum part payment.

  • If ‘Fees calculated on Payment Amount’ is set to False, then the minimum Part Payment is just the arrears amount.

  • If  there are multiple Ready Finance accounts allocated to different bailiffs and you enter a payment amount for one of the accounts in the ‘Combined Ready Finance’ screen, then the FEE field should be auto-calculated. Again the Fee amount calculated will depend upon different criteria such as the amount you have entered, the status of the account, and whether you are making a whole or part payment. The payment should only be processed against the account that you entered the payment amount for and all other Ready Finance accounts should remain the same as before the transaction.

 

Combined Ready Finance printing on Receipt & Payments Cards

 

  • When a Payment Amount is entered which is split across Ready Finance accounts, or an individual account (through the 'Combined Ready Finance Accounts' screen and a receipt is printed, the combined remaining balance across all Ready Finance accounts should be printed on the receipt. This also applies when the transactions are updated on the Payments Card.

  • When a new Payment Card is printed, the combined Agreement Total of the Ready Finance accounts should be displayed.

  • Individual statements on each Ready Finance account can be printed by selecting the account from the Accounts List (black arrow marks the highlighted account), and then selecting from the menu ‘Print' -> Statement of Account’. The details of that account should only be printed.

 

Payment Card printing

 

  • When entering the Payments screen and loading the accounts belonging to a Customer,if there are any unprinted transactions you should receive a prompt : “Unprinted Transactions for account number  ‘nnn-nnnn-nnnn-1’ Please enter Payment Card for printing at row number (1)”.

  • The Payment Card should display any unprinted transactions that were previously taken but were not printed. If the payments were taken on the same day then the sum of all payments taken on that day should be printed.  

  • If the account has been delivered and it is the first time you are entering the Payment screen since delivering the account you should receive the prompt: “Deliveries Outstanding – do you wish to update the payment card for the installment and due date? “

  • When printing a new Payment Card , the correct ‘Agreement Total’, ‘Customers Name and Address’, and ‘RF Credit Limit’ if it is a Ready Finance account should be displayed. If this is a HP account, then no RF Credit Limit should be printed.
