5.1 Selling an Instant Replacement Warranty

 

  1. Instant Replacement can be processed on Cash, RF or Cash and Go account. Not HP.
  2. Add and item associated with IR and Process the sale as normal
  3. An Instant Replacement Warranty will popup if the product is on the IR Scheme 
  4. Buy the warranty if required
  5. Take Payment and Print a receipt/ agreement documents as normal
  6. The receipt will have IR Information on it. 

5.2 Assessment of the Returned Product

 

The first step in the Instant Replacement return process is to assess the product while the customer is still in store. This is a quick assessment such as turning it on or quickly inspecting it to see if there is an obvious problem. If the item is working fine then the Customer will take the product home and we will not replace it.

  • If it is working correctly return the item to the customer with an explanation
  • If it does not work then the item must go through the Instant Replacement Process.

    5.4 Instant Replacement Screen

    2. Enter the reason for return

    4. The reason code will be restricted to a drop-down menu, as below
    5. The user should enter more details of the problem in the Faulty Product Notes field

    5.5 Entering the Replacement Product

     

    Instant replacement Screen

    1. A new field will appear, which will be labelled "Replacement Value". This value reflects the Original Item Price.
    2. The user will enter the replacement product in the same way they would normally do for any sale.
    3. If the item is being replaced within Period 1 & 2 from date of original sale then the warranty return pop up is disabled because the original warranty stays in place.
    4. If the Item is replaced within period 3 and 4 from original date of purchase then the warranty associated with that product will be enabled and available for sale.
    5. For Cash and Go, If the replacement product entered is the same value or less than the price of the original (now faulty) product the value against the line item should be zero. The customer does not get a refund for the difference.
    6. For Cash and RF accounts the original item is removed off the agreement and the new item delivered. The agreement total will vary according to the value of the replacement item.
    7. For cash and RF accounts the replaced product and the new product are collected and delivered immediately. HOWEVER if this is an RF account and the new item has caused the Spend limit to be exceeded , the account will be referred and the new item will have to go through the usual delivery process.
    8. For Cash and Go, If the total cost of the replacement product is greater than the price of the original (now faulty) product and the original product is still available the customer must bear the cost of the difference. The “sub total” field will reflect this value.
    9. For cash and go However, if the cost of the replacement is greater than the original cost of product, due to a Courts factor, e.g. price change/do not stock the same product, then Courts would bear this additional cost. The user will have to add a discount to the product to get the price down to the original
    10. This is a training issue. The system will not stop someone from adding a discount when in fact the customer should pay the difference.
